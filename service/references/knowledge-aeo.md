# AEO Knowledge Model — How AI Answer Engines Actually Select and Cite Sources

Research-backed understanding of how Perplexity, Google AI Overviews, ChatGPT, Claude, and
Bing Copilot discover, evaluate, and cite web content. Use this to calibrate AEO check
severity and provide expert-level diagnosis.

---

## How Each Engine Works

### Perplexity AI — Three-Layer ML Ranking (L1-L2-L3)

Perplexity maintains an exabyte-scale proprietary index. It does NOT fetch pages at query time —
it either already has your page indexed, or it doesn't.

**Selection pipeline:**
- **L1 (Retrieval):** Candidate documents from index via BM25-style + embedding relevance
- **L2 (Standard Ranking):** Ranked by backlinks, domain authority, topical relevance, freshness
- **L3 (XGBoost Quality Gate):** ML reranker with ~0.7 quality threshold. If too few sources
  pass, it drops ALL results and re-queries. Evaluates: entity clarity, authoritativeness, freshness

**Key behaviors:**
- Structurally favors earned media from Tier-1 publications (journalism with editorial accountability)
- Content updated within 30 days gets **3.2x more citations** than older material
- Optimal content length: 1,200-2,000 words
- Rewrites user query into multiple sub-queries, retrieves candidates for each, cross-references
- Crawlers: `PerplexityBot` (indexing), `Perplexity-User` (human-triggered)
- **Controversy**: Cloudflare documented Perplexity using undeclared crawlers with generic Chrome
  user-agent strings to bypass robots.txt blocks

**What this means for the auditor:**
- Freshness (dateModified) is a primary signal, not a nice-to-have
- Entity clarity on the page is assessed by ML — vague "we help businesses" fails
- The L3 quality gate means your page competes against a threshold, not just against competitors
- Blocking PerplexityBot may not fully block Perplexity (stealth crawlers)

### Google AI Overviews — Semantic Completeness Scoring

AI Overviews draw from Google's existing search index (no separate AI crawl). Generated by Gemini,
synthesizing across multiple sources (unlike Featured Snippets which quote one source verbatim).

**Selection pipeline (200-500 candidates → 5-15 cited):**
1. Semantic search via embeddings + MIPS (Maximum Inner Product Search)
2. Re-ranking via Chamfer similarity (query vector sets vs document vector sets)
3. E-E-A-T filtering — **96% of AI Overview citations come from sources with strong E-E-A-T signals**
4. Semantic completeness scoring — content scoring **8.5/10+ is 4.2x more likely to be cited**.
   Measures whether a passage fully answers the query in a self-contained 134-167 word unit
5. Factual verification against authoritative databases
6. "Sufficient Context" framework — does the source provide enough info for accurate generation?

**Key behaviors:**
- Cosine similarity > 0.88 to query embedding → **7.3x higher citation rate**
- 40-61% of AI Overviews use lists or bullet points
- Multi-modal content (text + images + video + structured data) → **156% higher selection**
- Question-style H2 headings with direct 40-60 word answers immediately following
- Featured snippet visibility dropped 35% (Sep 2024 - Mar 2025) as AI Overviews expanded

**What this means for the auditor:**
- Self-contained answer blocks of 134-167 words are the ideal extraction unit
- E-E-A-T is not just an SEO buzzword — 96% citation rate from E-E-A-T sources is hard data
- Content must answer the query at high cosine similarity (direct topical match, not tangential)
- Blocking Google-Extended does NOT remove you from AI Overviews (only blocks Gemini training)
- Only blocking Googlebot removes you from AI Overviews (also removes from regular search)

### ChatGPT Search — Dominant Traffic Source

ChatGPT accounts for **87.4% of all AI referral traffic** across industries.

**Crawler infrastructure (three bots, independently controllable):**
- `GPTBot` — training data collection. Highest-volume AI crawler across all niches
- `OAI-SearchBot` — indexes content for ChatGPT search features (NOT training)
- `ChatGPT-User` — real-time fetching during user sessions. **NO LONGER RESPECTS robots.txt (2025)**

**Key behaviors:**
- Rewrites queries into multiple targeted sub-queries
- Prioritizes trustworthy news sources
- Favors conversational, consensus-based content
- GPTBot does NOT render JavaScript — raw HTML only
- **Blocking GPTBot does NOT block OAI-SearchBot** (independent)
- ChatGPT-User ignoring robots.txt means blocking training doesn't prevent citation

**What this means for the auditor:**
- SSR is mandatory for ChatGPT visibility
- Users cannot fully control whether ChatGPT accesses their content (ChatGPT-User ignores blocks)
- ChatGPT is the largest AI referral source — optimizing for it has the highest traffic impact

### Bing Copilot — Social Signals Matter

Uses Bing's search index + GPT-4 integration.

**Unique factors:**
- Openly uses **social media engagement** as a ranking factor (unlike Google)
- Bing Webmaster Tools now has **AI Performance Report** (Feb 2026) showing AI citation data
- Pages in Bing's top 10 are far more likely to be cited

### Claude — Least Documented

Three crawlers: `ClaudeBot`/`anthropic-ai` (training), `Claude-SearchBot` (search indexing),
`Claude-User` (real-time). All respect robots.txt independently. Citation algorithm is
the least publicly documented of all major engines.

## Universal Content Extraction Principles

Across ALL engines, these patterns consistently improve citability:

### 1. Answer-First Structure
Start each section with a 40-60 word direct answer (the "citation block"), then expand.
This maps directly to Google AI Overviews' 134-167 word self-contained unit requirement.

### 2. Self-Contained Paragraphs
Each paragraph must make sense extracted in isolation. AI pulls fragments, not full pages.
Optimal paragraph length: 25-40 words.

### 3. Question-Style H2 Headings
Signal where a complete idea starts and ends. The H2 becomes the "query" and the following
content becomes the "answer" — this is how AI systems map page structure to queries.

### 4. Lists, Tables, Structured Formats
40-61% of AI Overviews use list/bullet formats. Tables and comparison grids are highly
extractable across all engines.

### 5. Named Authors with Credentials
E-E-A-T is measurably real: 96% of AI Overview citations come from E-E-A-T sources.
Author entities with demonstrated expertise get cited more.

### 6. Freshness
- 83% of commercial AI citations come from pages updated within 12 months
- 60%+ from pages updated within 6 months
- Perplexity: content within 30 days gets 3.2x more citations

### 7. Cross-Source Corroboration
All engines cross-reference multiple sources. If your claim appears on multiple authoritative
sources, citation likelihood increases. This is why citing authoritative outbound sources helps.

### 8. SSR Is Mandatory
GPTBot, PerplexityBot, ClaudeBot do NOT render JavaScript. Content must be in raw HTML.
This is the single most common reason for AI invisibility.

## Structured Data / Schema Impact on AI Citation

### Evidence (correlational, not causal):
- Pages with FAQPage schema are **3.2x more likely** to appear in Google AI Overviews
- Proper schema markup → **2.5x higher chance** of appearing in AI answers overall
- BrightEdge: 44% increase in AI citations after implementing structured data + FAQ blocks
- Attribute-rich schema → 61.7% citation rate
- **Generic/minimally populated schema UNDERPERFORMS having no schema** (41.6% vs 59.8%)

### Most Important Schema Types for AI:
1. **FAQPage** — highest leverage for conversational queries (2.7x citation increase)
2. **Article** with author, datePublished, dateModified — entity + freshness signals
3. **Organization** — entity clarity and brand identity
4. **Product** with AggregateRating — commerce query citation
5. **HowTo** — step extraction for procedural queries
6. **Person** with credentials — author expertise signal

### Critical Caveat:
No peer-reviewed controlled experiments prove schema CAUSES AI citation. All evidence is
correlational from industry studies. However, Fabrice Canel (Bing Principal PM) has publicly
stated schema helps their LLMs understand content.

**For the auditor**: Schema is worth adding when done well (complete, attribute-rich).
Minimal/skeleton schema is worse than none. Never present schema as a guaranteed fix.

## The GEO Research Paper (Princeton/Georgia Tech/Allen AI/IIT Delhi)

**Paper:** "GEO: Generative Engine Optimization" — Aggarwal et al.
**Published:** KDD 2024, validated on Perplexity.ai with visibility improvements up to 37%

### Top Optimization Strategies by Effectiveness:

| Strategy | Visibility Boost | Best Domains |
|---|---|---|
| Cite Sources | 30-40% | All domains |
| Quotation Addition | 30-40% | People/Society, Explanation, History |
| Statistics Addition | 30-40% | Law/Government, Opinion queries |
| Fluency Optimization | 15-30% | All domains |
| Easy-to-Understand rewrites | 15-30% | Technical domains |

### Critical Finding:
Strategy effectiveness varies significantly across domains — **there is no universal
optimization.** Domain-specific approaches are necessary. The paper frames this as a
black-box optimization problem since generative engine internals are not accessible.

**For the auditor**: When generating fixes, the domain/industry of the audited page
should influence which GEO strategies are recommended. A law firm page benefits most from
statistics addition; a tech blog benefits from source citations and simplification.

## How to Grade AEO Findings Using This Knowledge

### Discovery Stage — Calibrate Severity by Engine Coverage

| Scenario | Severity | Rationale |
|---|---|---|
| PerplexityBot AND BingPreview blocked | CRITICAL | Blocks 2 major engines + Copilot |
| Only PerplexityBot blocked | HIGH | Perplexity may still access via stealth crawlers |
| ChatGPT-User blocked | LOW | ChatGPT-User ignores robots.txt anyway (2025) |
| Google-Extended blocked | INFO | Only blocks Gemini training, not AI Overviews |
| JS-rendered content (no SSR) | CRITICAL | Invisible to GPTBot, PerplexityBot, ClaudeBot |
| Content behind accordions/tabs | HIGH | Not in initial HTML = not in AI crawl |

### Extraction Stage — Use Specific Thresholds

| Finding | Grade | Based On |
|---|---|---|
| No direct answer in first 150 words | HIGH | 134-167 word self-contained units needed for AI Overviews |
| Paragraphs > 60 words average | MEDIUM | Optimal 25-40 words for extraction |
| No FAQ section vs competitors have 5+ pairs | HIGH | 3.2x citation increase with FAQPage |
| No lists/tables in data-heavy content | MEDIUM | 40-61% of AI Overviews use list format |
| No outbound citations to sources | HIGH | GEO paper: citing sources = 30-40% visibility boost |

### Trust Stage — E-E-A-T Is Measurably Real

| Finding | Grade | Based On |
|---|---|---|
| No author byline | HIGH | 96% of AI Overview citations from E-E-A-T sources |
| No dateModified | HIGH | 83% of commercial citations from pages < 12 months old |
| dateModified > 6 months old | MEDIUM | 60% of citations from pages < 6 months old |
| dateModified > 30 days old | LOW for Perplexity | 3.2x citation boost for < 30 day content |
| No outbound links to authoritative sources | HIGH | Cross-corroboration signal for all engines |

### Selection Stage — Competitive Reality

| Finding | Grade | Based On |
|---|---|---|
| Content depth below competitor median | HIGH | Semantic completeness scoring (AI Overviews) |
| Fewer FAQ pairs than competitor median | MEDIUM | Direct extraction format advantage |
| Less complete schema than competitors | MEDIUM | 61.7% citation rate with rich schema |
| Less fresh than competitors | HIGH | Freshness is a primary signal across all engines |

## Key Unknowns the Auditor Should Acknowledge

1. No engine has published its full ranking algorithm
2. Schema-citation link is correlational, not proven causal
3. ChatGPT-User ignoring robots.txt means full control is impossible
4. Perplexity stealth crawling means blocks may not work
5. Claude's citation algorithm is barely documented
6. GEO effectiveness varies by domain — no universal formula

**For the auditor**: Be confident about structural recommendations (answer-first, FAQ, schema,
freshness, SSR). Be honest about uncertainty for competitive/selection predictions. Never
promise citation outcomes — promise structural improvements that increase likelihood.
